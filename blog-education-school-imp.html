Gamification in Education

- How to make School fun and improve the learning outcome

  • June 11, 2021

Gamification has been widely used in education and learning from many years. But how can it be used to ensure the student’s engagement and improve their learning outcome?

What is Gamification in Education?

The theory behind gamification in education is that a learner can learn their best when they are having fun. Their outcome would be best when they have to set their goals, when they get appreciated more, achieve targets, etc. This theory can also be applied in making School fun. By involving game elements such as healthy competition, points, badges, team work ; students get motivated to learn more.

Now a days, due to extensive digitization, it is extremely important for schools to switch from manual work to automated systems to reduce the work load. If this tedious task can be both automated and fun, the engagement of the user increases.

At school, the main focus is either academics or sports. When only these things are given the highest importance, the overall personality development is hampered. Students must be encouraged to follow their strong pursuit and this can be achieved when they get help to identify their strong and weak skills. When their skills are identified, they can hone their strong points and improve on their weak. This can be achieved only if learning is fun.

How to use Game Elements in Education?

School can implement gamification in various ways. By applying the following game elements, students would engage and enjoy.

1. Points, Badges and Leaderboards

Positive reinforcement learning theory states that when a desired or pleasant outcome is introduced after a specific action or behaviour, it’s more likely to reoccur.

When students are given positive rewards after every good action it stimulates a happy feeling, which in turn ensures that they follow rules. In this way schools can discipline their students.

2. Levels

Levels indicate growth. In games each level gets tougher and tougher indicating difficult to achieve targets. But once the level is finished a feeling of accomplishment is achieved, motivating us to keep playing.

When students are given a peer competitive environment, just the right amount of nudging and competition, they grow and realise their abilities. Also they are given a chance to explore new opportunities.

3. Social Skills

In this, students learn the importance of team work by gamification. By interacting more, they develop confidence and problems such as stage fear can be tackled.

Also when they showcase their talent or share their views, they feel that they are contributing to a greater cause.

4. Points in Non Academic context

Point system can work with non academic tasks such as extra-curricular activities, helping peers, tidying the classroom, etc. When student get points for every activity they perform, they feel excited and continue their good work.

5. In-depth Report card analysis

Using graphs and charts to project the detailed analysis of a student's behaviour and suggesting on how to improve better will help the student in engaging.

For eg; Helping the student realise that they are better at some things and a bit weaker in others. Along with this report, suggesting on how to improve at their weakness and train them to get better at their stronger points.

Benefits of Gamification

• Students get an opportunity to think outside the box. They get to explore new ideas.

• Their fear of making mistakes is lessened as they can try again.

• Progress indicators help them keep a track of what they have learnt.

• Higher engagement and concentration.

• As students love games, this gaming environment will make them more proactive.

• Students help each other grow.

• And lastly, it’s Fun!!!
