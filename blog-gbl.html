Game-Based Learning

- Enhancing the learning process

  • June 25, 2021

Games have been present from a long time in all cultures. They are played solely for enjoyment purpose. However playing games has numerous benefits such as improving patience, problem-solving, social skills, strategizing, etc. Let’s see how inculcating games in learning improves the learning process!

What is Game-Based Learning (GBL) ?

Game-Based Learning is the use of game elements with educational goals to support a student’s growth and development. The GBL technique focuses on teaching through failure, repetition and accomplishment of goals. In games, a player usually starts with easy levels and gradually attains mastery. While attaining mastery the player may fail and might have to repeat the level, learn from the mistakes made in that level. In learning too, this method can be applied.

Students work on their goal, choose their actions and learn from the consequences of their actions. This results in active learning rather than passive learning. In a GBL environment, students learn new concepts and can practice their skills in a risk-free setting. Their progress in the game is directly related to their understanding of the subject being taught.

How to implement GBL ?

1. Board Games

Students still play board games, card games, and many others. These tend to emphasize strategy elements rather than being completely random games of chance. Monopoly can be considered an educational game. It has all the necessary elements: a story, characters, points, competition, and many other aspects. There are many examples of Monopoly-like games for schools with modified rules for different subjects, like History Monopoly or Math Monopoly.

2. Digital Games

A digital game does not involve skills that are connected to the use of the body and the real space, but it can train students to collaborate in a different and virtual way. In this type of game, students can be involved in the construction of the game, especially if the teacher is not able to manage online tools without their help. Students have a character (but not necessarily an account) that moves through the game where they face challenges that are placed along the games’ path.
Examples: Universe Sandbox, Minecraft, Math Blaster, etc.

3. Real life Games

This is probably the most motivating, but also the most stressful type of game. In this kind, students must move, act, use their body and their minds in order to play. This is the most immersive type and it provokes students in almost every aspect of their learning.

Since there is the possibility to move into a certain space, the real life game is often connected to the theatre. It’s easy to find role play activities, as well as simulations or drama in this type of game learning. Students act “as if they were” a character of the game, make decisions according to their goals, the environment, and the rules.

Benefits of GBL

• Motivation
Motivation is the key to good teaching and learning flow in the classroom. For this reason, GBL is very effective, as games are naturally motivating and engaging. Moreover, games provide something that can be described as “far from a traditional lesson” and they are often include competition among students; which can raise the level of motivation in a classroom.

• Self confidence
As students are appreciated more, they feel a sense of accomplishment and hence this boosts their self confidence.

• Enhances real world skills
As GBL promotes ‘learn by doing’, students understand the real world applications better.

• Memory
GBL is known to sharpen the student’s memory as learning concepts through visual leads to better retention power. It also leads to decrease in the learning time and faster concept grabbing for students.

• Creativity
GBL and games are effective tools for fostering creativity, especially if students are involved in the construction of the game. Imagination has no limits.

• Team work
It is not difficult to understand why a game is highly suitable for team and group work. Unity is strength and everyone loses or wins when they play a game together.
